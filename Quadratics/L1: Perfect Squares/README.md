# L1: Expansion and Simplification of Perfect Squares

## Part 1: Identifying

A perfect square looks like this: $(a+b)^2$. And it can be expanded to this: $a^2+2ab+b^2$. Do not get it confused with $(a \cdot b)^2$ because $(a+b)^2$ is not $a^2 + b^2$

## Part 2: Expanding

### Positives $(a+b)^2$
When expanding a positive perfect square, you substitute in the values per the formula $a^2+2ab+b^2$. For example:

Expand $(x+5)^2$:

**Step 1 - Square the First Term**

$x^2$

**Step 2 - Double the Product of A and B**

$2(x)(5) = 10x$

**Step 3 - Square the Last Term**

$5^2 = 25$

**Answer**

$x^2 + 10x + 25$

---

### Negatives $(a-b)^2$
When expanding a negative perfect square, you do the same as with a positive one and substitute in the values. However, there is one small difference: the formula now becomes $a^2-2ab+b^2$. Notice the first operation is now a minus, but the second is still a plus. Example:

Expand $(x-5)^2$:

**Step 1 - Square the First Term**

$x^2$

**Step 2 - Double the Product of A and B**

$2(x)(5) = 10x$

**Step 3 - Square the Last Term**

$5^2 = 25$

**Answer**

$x^2 - 10x + 25$

---

## Part 3: Simplifying
Simplifying, while less common compared to expanding, is still worth learning

### Positives
The quickest way to simplify a positive perfect square is using the following formula: $\sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}$. In an example:

Simplify $x^2+8x+16$

**Step 1: Identify the Variables**

$a = x^2$
$b = 16$

**Step 2: Substitute into Formula**

$\sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}$

$\sqrt{x^2} + \sqrt{16}$

**Step 3: Evaluate**

$\sqrt{x^2} + \sqrt{16}$

$x + 4$

Then add the square root to finish off:

**Answer**

$(x + 4)^2$

---

### Negatives
Simplifying negative perfect squares is the same as simplifying positive ones, apart from a slight difference: When simplifying a negative perfect square, the middle term is negative, so the binomial uses a minus sign (the formula also changes to be a negative: $\sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b}$)

Simplify $x^2-8x+16$ (notice the first operation is a minus now)

**Step 1: Identify the Variables**

$a = x^2$
$b = 16$

**Step 2: Substitute into Formula**

$\sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b}$

$\sqrt{x^2} - \sqrt{16}$

**Step 3: Evaluate**

$\sqrt{x^2} + \sqrt{16}$

$x - 4$

Then add the square root to finish off:

**Answer**

$(x - 4)^2$

---

### Coefficients
On the occasion where the integers (whole numbers) have a HCF, we can factorise them, such as:

$4y^2 + 24y + 36$

We can turn this equation to:

$4(y^2+6y+9)$

We then solve the inner brackets normally, and at the very end, we add back the factorised number, in this case, the brackets, when simplified, turn into:

$(y+3)^2$

Now we add back the factorised 4:

$4(y+3)^2$

> Remember not to put the factorised number inside the brackets, keep it **outside!**

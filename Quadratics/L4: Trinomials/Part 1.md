# L4: Trinomials Part 1

## Part 1: Identifying

To recognise a trinomial, look for an algebraic expression with **exactly three terms** separated by plus or minus signs. Each term can be a number, a variable, or a product of both. For example, $x^2 + 5x + 6$ is a trinomial because it has three parts: (x^2), (5x), and (6).

## Part 2: Factorising

Factoring trinomials can be difficult and time-consuming. Here's a step-by-step, very easy guide to how to factor them :)

**Step 1 - Understand Variables:**

A trinomial is written in the form $x^2 + bx + c$ (where `b` is an integer and `c` is a constant). To factor it, you first need to find 2 numbers that multiply to `c` and add to `b`. For example:

$x^2 + 5x + 6$

Think to yourself: *What are the factors of 6?*

- 1 and 6
- 2 and 3

Both multiply to 6. Now, they must fulfill the condition that they add to `b`

- 1 + 6 $\neq$ 5 ❌
- 3 + 2 = 5 ✅

**Step 2 - Put into Brackets:**

Now, you put each of those numbers (3 and 2) into brackets. Since both 3 and 2 are positive numbers, you add them to x. So the final formula is:

- (x+m)(x+n)

Sub in Values:

(x+3)(x+2)
> [IMPORTANT] Order **does not** matter!

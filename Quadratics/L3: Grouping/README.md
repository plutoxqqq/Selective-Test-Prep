# L3: Grouping

## Part 1: Identifying 

Grouping, although looking intimidating at first sight, is quite easy once you get the hang of it. Equations that require grouping to solve are usually 4 terms. Such as $ax + ay + bx + by$

## Part 2: Factorising

To factorise these types of expressions, you first need to split them into 2 separate parts:

**Step 1 - Split Into 2 Separate Terms**

$2x^2 + 4x + 3x + 6$

As we can see:

$(2x^2 + 4x) + (3x + 6)$

**Step 2 - Determine HCF of Each Part and Factor**

$(2x^2 + 4x)$ - We can see, in this section, the highest common factor (HCF), is $2x$:

$2x(x + 2)$

The above factorisation was done due to these simple factors: $2x^2$ means $2xx$, so taking out $2x$ be $2/2 = 1$, and $xx/x = x$. Then move the factored equation $(2x)$. To the outside, therefore resulting in the section above

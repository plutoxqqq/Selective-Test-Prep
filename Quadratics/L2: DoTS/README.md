# L2: DoTS

## Part 1: Identifying

DoTS are much easier to solve compared to perfect squares. DoTS come in 2 main forms: $(a+b)(a-b)$, where `a` and `b` are both integers, and $a^2 - b^2$.

## Part 2: Simplifying
When simplifying a DoTS, the simplest way is straightforward:

Simplify $x^2 - 81$

**Step 1 - Square Root Each Term**

$a = x^2$

$b = 81$

$\sqrt{x^2} = x$

$\sqrt{81} = 9$

**Step 2 - Write it Out**

$a^2 - b^2$

$x^2 - 9^2$

**Answer**

$x^2 - 9^2$

As simple as that!

---

## Part 3: Expanding
While less seen as compared to simplifying, the knowledge is still useful when doing topics like rationalisation, so it's worth learning:

Expand $(x+4)(x-4)

**Step 1 - Identify Variables**

$a = x$

$b = 4$

**Step 2 - Write in DoTS Form**

$a^2 - b^2$

$x^2 - 4^2$

$x^2 - 16$

---

### Coefficients
When simplifying a DoTS, and you come accross a HCF, you just factor it out. Ensure to always look for HCFs to factor out before solving:

Simplfiy $2x^2 - 162$

**Step 1 - Factor out HCF**

HCF of 2 and 162 = 2

So:

$2(x^2 - 81)

Then just solve as you would, but don't forget to put back the factored `2` at the start:

$2(x-9)(x+9)
